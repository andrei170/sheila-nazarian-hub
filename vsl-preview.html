Phase 1

Sheila Nazarian | VSL Preview Script

Section-by-section preview script for the 5-7 minute VSL above the Nazarian Plastic Surgery consultation request form (Funnel Blueprint Piece 02). Operator-grounded, regulated-medical register, no efficacy claims, no outcome promises, no procedure pricing.

The voice on screen is Dr. Nazarian's. AG1 directs the cut, writes the script, places the on-page embed. Dr. Nazarian's existing on-camera register (Netflix, IG, press) is the calibration anchor.

VSL Specification

Field Value
Length 5-7 minutes
Surface Nazarian Plastic Surgery practice site, above the consultation request form
Aspect 16:9 horizontal, captioned (English first, Spanish optional in v2)
Voice Dr. Nazarian on camera, single-presenter format
Setting Practice interior at 120 S Spalding Dr (preferred), professional studio (alternative)
Production Single recording day, B-roll from existing practice footage where appropriate (no patient before-and-after footage in the cut)
Distribution Embedded on the practice site hero, repurposable on the Spa26 site as a brand-stack walkthrough, repurposable on the Netflix-audience capture surface

Section-By-Section Outline (5-7 min)

Section 1 - Open (0:00 - 0:35)

Dr. Nazarian on camera at the practice.

"I am Dr. Sheila Nazarian, board-certified plastic surgeon, founder of Nazarian Plastic Surgery in Beverly Hills since 2013. If you landed here, you are at the point where you are weighing whether a consultation with our practice is the right next step. This video walks you through what the consultation actually covers, who it is for, who it is not for, and what to expect from our team. There is no procedure pitch in this video. There is no result-promise in this video. The consultation is where the right plan for you gets surfaced, and this video is the prep-step before that conversation."

Section 2 - The practice philosophy (0:35 - 1:20)

"Our hero positioning is Beauty With Integrity. That means Transparency, Honesty, and Authenticity in every step of the consultation, the planning, and the procedure. For our team, that translates to: we tell you what is on the table, we tell you what is not on the table for you, and we walk you through the trade-offs of every option before any decision is made. The consultation is not a sales step. The consultation is the diagnostic step."

Section 3 - Who the consultation is for (1:20 - 2:10)

"The consultation at our practice is for the patient who has thought about this for a while and is at the stage of wanting a board-certified plastic surgeon to walk them through their options. It is for the patient who wants the in-person or virtual diagnostic conversation before any decision is made. It is for the patient who values the medical-credential framing over the influencer framing. If you are at the very beginning of your aesthetic journey and the home-care or non-surgical step is where you are starting, the right step might be Spa26 (our med spa) or The Skin Spot (our home-regimen surface), and the team can route you accordingly."

Section 4 - What the consultation actually covers (2:10 - 3:30)

"In a typical consultation, we cover six things. One: your medical history and what brings you in. Two: the area or areas you want to discuss. Three: the surgical and non-surgical options on the table for what you are considering. Four: the trade-offs of every option, including the no-procedure option. Five: the recovery framework for any procedure that is on the table. Six: the next steps if you choose to move forward, including the planning visit, the pre-op steps, and the post-op care framework. The consultation does not commit you to anything. The consultation gives you the full picture."

Section 5 - In-person versus virtual (3:30 - 4:15)

"We offer in-person consultations at our office at 120 South Spalding Drive in Beverly Hills. We also offer virtual consultations for patients out of state or international. The in-person consultation gives the team the diagnostic depth that comes with the physical visit. The virtual consultation is the right starting point for patients who are weighing whether the in-person trip is the right next step. Either way, the consultation is the same diagnostic conversation; the format is the difference."

Section 6 - What to expect after the form (4:15 - 5:00)

"When you fill the consultation request form below this video, you will hear from the team within one business day. We will confirm the in-person or virtual format, the scheduling window, and any pre-consultation paperwork that helps the team prepare for your specific conversation. The consultation itself runs around forty-five minutes. After the consultation, the team follows up with the summary of what was covered and the plan, if a plan was discussed."

Section 7 - The footer note (5:00 - 5:45)

"A note on what this video is and what it is not. This video is an educational walkthrough of the consultation process. This video is not regulated medical advice. Cosmetic procedures carry inherent risks. Outcomes vary by patient and procedure. The consultation is where the right plan for you gets surfaced. If you are ready to start that conversation, fill the form below this video. If you are not ready yet, the home-regimen or non-surgical step might be the right starting point, and the practice site has links to both."

Section 8 - Close (5:45 - 6:30)

"From the team at Nazarian Plastic Surgery, thank you for the consideration. The form below this video is the next step when you are ready. I will see you at the consultation."

End frame: practice logo, address, phone number, "Beauty With Integrity" tagline.

What The VSL Does Not Do

  • Show patient before-and-after footage
  • Make any procedure-efficacy claim
  • Make any cosmetic-outcome promise
  • Quote any procedure pricing
  • Use any superlative ranking ("top," "best," "leading")
  • Use household-tier-name comparisons
  • Use em-dashes in the captions or the on-screen text
  • Use minute-quantified CTAs beyond the natural "around forty-five minutes" reference inside the educational walkthrough (the on-page CTA is the form)

What Gets Filmed In One Day

Take Content Duration

Take 2 Sections 3-4 in one continuous frame 2 min
Take 3 Sections 5-6 in one continuous frame 1.5 min
Take 4 Sections 7-8 (footer note + close) in one continuous frame 1.5 min
Pickup Phrase-level pickups for any line that needs a clean retake 30 min total

Total film day: 4-6 hours including setup. The cut is delivered within 5 business days of the film day.

VSL Voice Calibration Notes

  • Same operator-credential register Dr. Nazarian uses in press appearances and on Netflix
  • Direct, observational, evidence-grounded
  • No softening warmth that breaks the medical-credential frame
  • No clinical coldness that breaks the patient-respect frame
  • Steady cadence, no urgency, no scarcity framing
  • The voice carries the practice positioning; the script does not invent a new voice
