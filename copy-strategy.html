Foundation

Sheila Nazarian | Copy Strategy

Voice rules, niche bans, register separation, hero claims locked across the package.

The Voice

Operator-grounded, surgical-credential register, patient-respectful. Direct without being casual. Educational without being clinical-only. The voice carries the practice's stated hero positioning ("Beauty With Integrity Transparency Honesty Authenticity") and stays inside the regulated-medical-niche guardrails on every public surface.

Address form in cold first-touch and prospect-facing copy: Dr. Nazarian. "Sheila" is reserved for reciprocal informality after the first dialogue lands. The first-touch DM, the first email, the hub, and the landing all use "Dr. Nazarian."

Register Separation

Register Where it lives What it sounds like
Operator-grounded Hub, landing, email body, VSL script Sharp, numbers up front, observable-fact framing, no influencer warmth, no clinical promise
Regulated-medical Footer disclaimers, scorecard readouts, welcome-flow emails Educational, journey-stage, fit-and-readiness, "consult a board-certified professional"
Practice-aesthetic Visual layer of the hub and landing Neutral palette, gold accent, editorial typography, no influencer hot-tones

These three registers do not blend. The operator-grounded copy is the spine; the regulated-medical register frames every readout and disclaimer; the practice-aesthetic carries the visual surface.

Banned Vocabulary On Every AG1-Built Surface

Banned for medical-regulatory reasons

  • "Guaranteed results"
  • "Best in the world"
  • "Top surgeon" or any superlative ranking claim about Dr. Nazarian
  • "Transform your face / body / life"
  • "Look 10 years younger" or any age-quantified claim
  • "Pain-free" or "no downtime"
  • Any specific aesthetic outcome promise tied to any procedure
  • Any before-and-after framing inside AG1-built copy
  • Any pricing display on individual procedures

Banned for AG1 register

  • Game-changer, revolutionary, unlock your potential, transform your life
  • In today's fast-paced world, navigate the complexities, delve into
  • Paradigm shift, synergy, leverage synergies, harness the power
  • Seamlessly, effortlessly, elevate your, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, next-level
  • "Took a fresh pass" or any agency-shop jargon variant
  • "Quick question" or any M.A.C.E mass-cold-email cliche

Banned for cross-brand confusion

  • Any reference to a "cohort" or "course" for this prospect (Dr. Nazarian does not sell a course; AG1 does not propose one)
  • Any reference to a "mentorship" tier (regulatory and brand mismatch)
  • Any framing that suggests AG1 is making medical decisions or recommending procedures
  • Any household-tier-name comparison (the work stands on its own observable surface)

Sentence Discipline

  • Short, declarative, observable-fact framing
  • Numbers up front
  • One concept per sentence
  • Hyphens for ranges, colons for lists, sentence breaks for emphasis
  • Zero em-dashes anywhere across the package; the only exception is the literal sign-off pattern "- Andrei"
  • Hemingway grade 6 or below for the DM, the hub hero, the landing hero
  • Slightly higher grade tolerated for the research file, the bottleneck analysis, and the funnel blueprint (operator-doc register)

The Cross-Brand Naming Convention

When the copy references the six brands together, the order is fixed:

  1. Nazarian Plastic Surgery (the founding surface, the surgical practice)
  2. Spa26 (the med spa)
  3. The Skin Spot (the e-commerce platform, NazarianSkin is consolidated under this)
  4. Physique26 (wellness optimization)
  5. Nazarian Institute (non-profit, mentioned only where contextually relevant)
  6. Netflix series "Skin Decision: Before and After" (the top-of-funnel asset)

Where the copy references three of the six (the active commercial trio), the order is: Nazarian Plastic Surgery, Spa26, The Skin Spot. This is the operating cluster the cross-brand handoff architecture stitches.

What Stays Out Of The Copy

  • The Nazarian Institute non-profit mission framing as a sales lever
  • Dr. Nazarian's public advocacy work (antisemitism, Iran, women in medicine) as a sales lever
  • Any household-tier-name comparison
  • Any reference to internal vault filenames or memory entries
  • Any minute-quantified call CTA in the cold first-touch outreach files (the hub link is the only CTA there)
  • Any explicit AG1 pricing on the landing page (hint language only)
  • Any "$" character on the landing page (price hinting in words only)

Cross-Surface Tone Lock

The hub, the landing, the email body, the DMs, and the VSL preview script all read as if Dr. Nazarian's surgical-credential register and AG1's operator-grounded register were the same voice. AG1's voice respects the medical surface; the medical surface respects AG1's directness. Neither softens for the other. The blend is the surface.
