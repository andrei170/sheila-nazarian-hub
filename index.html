The Five Pieces

Five installs. Sit on top of the existing brand stack.

No clinical framework changes. No procedure menu changes. No pricing changes. No brand pivot. The five pieces patch the gaps between the audience, the buy decision, and the cross-brand handoff.

Piece 01 · The Cross-Brand Handoff Spine
01

The handoff architecture between Nazarian Plastic Surgery, Spa26, and The Skin Spot.

Six brands sit on six separate surfaces. A Skin Spot buyer does not get a Spa26 invite. A Spa26 booking does not get a Skin Spot home-regimen handoff. A Plastic Surgery consultation does not get a Skin Spot or Spa26 maintenance sequence. The fix is the cross-brand handoff: every Spa26 booking triggers a post-treatment Skin Spot home-regimen sequence; every Skin Spot purchase over a threshold triggers a Spa26 in-person upgrade invite; every Plastic Surgery consultation triggers a Skin Spot or Spa26 maintenance sequence depending on the procedure interest. Webhook handlers across the three booking and order systems. No procedure changes, no pricing changes, no clinical framework changes.

We build the webhook handlers, the tag rules, the routing logic, and the cross-brand email templates. The brands stay untouched. The handoff is the install.

Stitches the active commercial trio into one captured funnel without changing any of the underlying offers.

Piece 02 · The Practice VSL
02

The 5-7 minute VSL above the Nazarian Plastic Surgery consultation request form.

The consultation decision currently happens on a static hero with rotating images. No video. No founder voice above the form. The 225K @themodelsurgeon audience and the 1M @drsheilanazarian audience that land on the practice site decide whether to fill the consultation form based on text and image alone. The fix is a single-presenter VSL in the practice voice ("Beauty With Integrity Transparency Honesty Authenticity") walking the consultation process, the in-person versus virtual difference, and the post-consultation pathway. No procedure-result claim. No outcome promise. No before-and-after framing.

We write the script, direct the record, edit the cut, embed above the consultation form, set captions, and wire the view-completion events. The voice on screen is Dr. Nazarian's.

Lifts the consultation-form fill on existing practice-site traffic. Repurposes across Spa26 and the Netflix-audience capture surface with light edits.

Piece 03 · The Readiness Scorecard
03

The "Where Are You In Your Aesthetic Journey?" scorecard with email capture.

The BuildMyBod estimator on the practice site estimates procedure cost. It does not measure readiness, fit, or aesthetic-journey stage, and it does not route the visitor to the right brand surface. The fix is a 60-90 second scorecard with email capture, four readiness bands (Curious-and-researching / Ready-for-non-surgical / Ready-for-surgical-consultation / Returning-patient-maintenance), branching logic, and routing rules. Captures email at the top, returns a three-line readout in the practice voice, routes each band to the right brand surface (Skin Spot home regimen for curious, Spa26 booking for non-surgical, practice VSL plus consultation form for surgical, returning-patient sequence for returning).

We build the scorecard question logic, the scoring algorithm, the readout copy in the regulated-medical register, the routing rules, the email tagging, and the on-page surfaces.

Filters and segments cold traffic before any booking step. Lifts the right downstream brand surface for the right journey-stage band. Captures email at scale for the first time.

Piece 04 · The Captured-Email Backbone
04

The aggregated email database and the segmented welcome flow per buyer tier.

Six brands run six separate (or zero) email systems. A Skin Spot newsletter signup does not see a Spa26 launch. A Plastic Surgery consultation request does not see a Skin Spot drop. The fix is a unified captured-email backbone fed by the scorecard, the consultation form, the Spa26 booking, the Skin Spot newsletter, and the speaker-site contact form. Webhook handlers tag every contact by brand-of-origin, scorecard band, and purchase status. Segmented welcome flows per buyer tier: Spa26 first-time-booking, Spa26 returning-patient, Skin Spot first-purchase, Skin Spot recurring-buyer, Plastic Surgery consultation-requested, Plastic Surgery post-consultation. Cross-brand routing inside the welcome flow surfaces the next-logical brand at the right cadence.

We wire the webhook handler architecture, the tag rules, the segmentation logic, the per-tier welcome templates, and the cross-brand routing inside the welcome flow.

Captures the upgrade path the existing buyer base does not currently have. Builds the warmest possible audience for any future paid-media spend.

Piece 05 · The Netflix-Audience Capture Surface
05

The series-anchored capture surface and the speaker-site routing layer.

Skin Decision is the single largest top-of-funnel asset Nazarian Plastic Surgery owns. Eight episodes, Emmy-nominated, on Netflix since 2020. The Netflix-driven audience routes to a link-in-bio aggregator today. The speaker site (sheilanazarian.com) lists the six brands as text references, not as a routed handoff. The fix is a dedicated capture surface that opens with a series-anchored framing in the speaker voice, surfaces the scorecard at the top, source-tags the email by Netflix origin, and routes the band to the right brand surface. The speaker-site routing layer beneath this replaces the six-brand text references with routed brand cards.

We build the Netflix-audience capture surface, the speaker-site routing layer, the routed brand cards, and the source-tagged email entry that feeds the captured-email backbone.

Aggregates the largest unbuilt segment of the audience into the captured-email backbone. Routes the Netflix-driven visitor to the right brand surface with a pre-loaded series-anchored context.

Week 9-10

Piece 05. Netflix-audience capture surface and speaker-site routing. Series-anchored landing, routed brand cards, Netflix-source email tagging.

A Note On Register

Regulated-medical-niche register, every surface.

The cosmetic and aesthetic-medicine space carries clear regulatory guardrails. Every public surface in this package stays at the level of educational content, journey-stage guidance, and patient-fit framing. No income claims. No cosmetic-outcome promises. No procedure-result framing. No before-and-after content inside AG1-built copy. No procedure pricing on any AG1-built surface. The scorecard surfaces readiness, not a clinical clearance. The welcome flow routes by brand-of-origin, not by predicted outcome. The cross-brand handoff is a navigation system, not a treatment plan. The AG1-built surfaces sit underneath Dr. Nazarian's existing audience-facing register, complementing the practice positioning, not replacing it.
