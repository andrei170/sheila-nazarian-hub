Lead Doc

Sheila Nazarian | Bottleneck Analysis

Lead doc. Five-piece structural map. Operator-grounded, regulated-medical register, no income claims.

The One-Sentence Read

Six brands. 2M+ aggregate social audience. Zero observable cross-brand handoff infrastructure, no VSL above any consultation or product checkout, no readiness-or-fit scorecard above any booking step, no buyer-tier segmented welcome flow underneath any purchase event, and no captured-email aggregation across the Netflix-driven personal IG, the practice IG, and the speaker-site reach.

The brands work. The bridges between them do not exist yet.

The Five Pieces

Piece 01 - The cross-brand handoff architecture between Nazarian Plastic Surgery, Spa26, The Skin Spot, Physique26, and the Netflix audience

Why it matters. Six brands sit on six separate surfaces. A Skin Spot buyer does not get a Spa26 invite. A Spa26 booking does not get a Skin Spot home-regimen handoff. A practice consultation does not get a Skin Spot post-op skincare regimen sequence. The Netflix audience routes to a link-in-bio aggregator instead of a structured cross-brand surface that captures email and routes by interest.

The fix. A cross-brand routing architecture that stitches the six brands without changing any of the underlying offers.

  • Hop A: Netflix-driven IG audience and speaker-site visitors route to a "Where Are You In Your Aesthetic Journey?" readiness-and-fit scorecard with email capture. The scorecard returns one of four readiness bands and routes the lead to the right brand surface.
  • Hop B: Every Spa26 booking gets a post-treatment Skin Spot home-regimen email sequence. Every Skin Spot purchase over a threshold gets a Spa26 in-person upgrade invite. Every Plastic Surgery consultation gets a post-consultation Skin Spot or Spa26 maintenance sequence depending on the procedure interest.
  • Hop C: A monthly cross-brand newsletter aggregates the captured email list and routes the audience to the brand surface most relevant to that month's content (Netflix-anniversary content, Spa26 launches, Skin Spot drops, Plastic Surgery educational content).

What AG1 builds. The cross-brand handoff architecture. Webhook handlers across the practice booking system, Spa26 booking system, and the Skin Spot Shopify event stream. Routing rules. Email sequences per hop. The aggregated cross-brand newsletter template and cadence. The brand surfaces themselves and the procedures themselves stay untouched.

What AG1 does not touch. Procedure recommendations, medical decisions, clinical efficacy claims, treatment outcomes, cosmetic-result language, pricing on individual procedures.

Piece 02 - The 5-7 minute VSL above the Nazarian Plastic Surgery consultation request

Why it matters. The consultation decision currently happens on a static hero with rotating images. No video. No operator-grounded walkthrough. No founder voice above the consultation form. The 225K @themodelsurgeon audience and the 1M @drsheilanazarian audience that land on the practice site decide whether to fill the consultation form based on text and image alone.

The fix. A 5-7 minute VSL filmed in the practice's existing register ("Beauty With Integrity Transparency Honesty Authenticity") embedded above the consultation request form on nazarianplasticsurgery.com.

The VSL is not a sales pitch. It is an educational walkthrough of the practice philosophy, the consultation process, the in-person and virtual consultation difference, the post-consultation pathway, and the operator voice that Dr. Nazarian already brings to Netflix and to public appearances.

What AG1 builds. The VSL script, the production direction, the edit, the embed, the captions, the on-page placement, and the variant tracking. The voice on screen is Dr. Nazarian's.

What AG1 does not touch. Any medical claim, any outcome promise, any procedure-result framing, any pricing display.

Piece 03 - The "Where Are You In Your Aesthetic Journey?" readiness-and-fit scorecard

Why it matters. Every visitor across the six brand surfaces enters cold and unsegmented. A first-time interested visitor and a returning Spa26 patient and a Skin Spot recurring buyer all enter the same way. The BuildMyBod Price Estimator on the practice site estimates procedure cost. It does not measure readiness, fit, or aesthetic-journey stage, and it does not route the visitor to the right brand surface.

The fix. A 60-90 second scorecard with email capture, branching logic, four readiness bands, and routing rules.

  • Bands: Curious-and-researching / Ready-for-non-surgical / Ready-for-surgical-consultation / Returning-patient-maintenance.
  • Each band returns a 3-line readout in the practice voice (educational, regulated-medical register, no efficacy promises).
  • Each band routes to the right surface: practice consultation form for the surgical band, Spa26 booking for the non-surgical band, Skin Spot home-regimen recommendations for the curious band, returning-patient maintenance sequence for the returning band.
  • Email captured at the top of the scorecard, tagged by band, feeds the cross-brand routing architecture from Piece 01.

What AG1 builds. The scorecard question logic, the scoring algorithm, the readout copy in the practice voice, the routing rules, the email tagging, the on-page surface where the scorecard lives.

What AG1 does not touch. Procedure recommendations, medical fitness decisions, clinical eligibility. The scorecard surfaces readiness and routes to a human consultation; it does not diagnose, prescribe, or clear a patient for any procedure.

Piece 04 - The captured-email aggregation and the segmented welcome flow per brand

Why it matters. Six brands run six separate (or zero) email systems. A Skin Spot newsletter signup does not see a Spa26 launch. A Plastic Surgery consultation request does not see a Skin Spot Black Friday drop. There is no single aggregated owned channel that holds the 2M+ social audience as captured email and segments them by buyer type, brand interaction, or aesthetic-journey stage.

The fix. A unified captured-email backbone with brand-tier segmentation underneath.

  • A single aggregated email database fed by the scorecard, the practice consultation form, the Spa26 booking form, the Skin Spot newsletter signup, and the speaker-site contact form.
  • Webhook handlers tag every contact by brand-of-origin, purchase status, scorecard band, and (where the platform supports it) procedure interest.
  • Segmented welcome flows per buyer tier: cold-scorecard-lead, Spa26 first-time-booking, Spa26 returning-patient, Skin Spot first-purchase, Skin Spot recurring-buyer, Plastic Surgery consultation-requested, Plastic Surgery post-consultation.
  • Cross-brand routing inside the welcome flow surfaces the next-logical brand at the right cadence (Skin Spot home-regimen after a Spa26 booking, Spa26 maintenance after a Plastic Surgery consultation, Plastic Surgery educational content after a Skin Spot purchase over a threshold).

What AG1 builds. The webhook handler architecture, the tag rules, the segmentation logic, the per-tier welcome templates, and the cross-brand routing inside the welcome flow.

What AG1 does not touch. The Skin Spot product catalog, the Spa26 service menu, the practice procedure offering, or any inventory or scheduling system underneath the brand surfaces.

Piece 05 - The Netflix-audience capture surface and the speaker-site routing

Why it matters. Skin Decision: Before and After is the single largest top-of-funnel asset Dr. Nazarian owns. Eight episodes, Emmy-nominated, on Netflix since 2020. The series drives audience to the personal IG (1M followers). The audience that arrives via the series does not see a structured capture surface that routes them across the six brands. The speaker site (sheilanazarian.com) lists all six brands as text references, not as a routed handoff.

The fix. A Netflix-audience capture surface that lives on the speaker-site domain or as a dedicated landing.

  • The surface opens with a Netflix-anniversary or series-recap framing in the speaker voice.
  • The visitor enters the scorecard from Piece 03 at the top of the surface.
  • The four readiness bands route the Netflix-driven visitor to the right brand surface with a pre-loaded context (the "you came from the Netflix audience" framing in the welcome email).
  • The surface aggregates the Netflix-driven audience into the captured-email backbone from Piece 04, tagged by source.

The speaker-site routing layer beneath this surfaces the six brands as routed cards, not text references. Each brand card opens to a one-paragraph context and a primary CTA that routes through the right downstream flow.

What AG1 builds. The Netflix-audience capture surface, the speaker-site routing layer, the routed brand cards, and the email tagging for the Netflix-source visitor.

What AG1 does not touch. The Netflix series IP, the show's media-rights framing, or any licensing language. The capture surface references the series as observable public fact; it does not re-license or re-frame the show creative.

The Install Cadence

Five pieces. Each one installs in under two weeks per piece from kickoff. The cross-brand handoff architecture (Piece 01) is the spine - it benefits from being installed second so the scorecard (Piece 03) and the captured-email backbone (Piece 04) feed it from day one. Recommended sequence: 03 -> 04 -> 01 -> 02 -> 05.

What Does Not Get Built In This Plan

  • A new course, a new mentorship, a new cohort, or a new product line of any kind. Dr. Nazarian already runs six brands. The plan adds infrastructure around the existing offers, never adds an offer.
  • Any medical recommendation, any procedure-efficacy claim, any cosmetic-outcome promise, any pricing display on individual procedures.
  • Any modification to the Netflix series, the show creative, or the licensing framework.
  • Any change to the practice's clinical, regulatory, or compliance posture.
  • Any creative or brand-aesthetic change unless explicitly scoped on day one of the install.

Year-1 Floor Framing

The five builds are conversion infrastructure for the offers Dr. Nazarian already runs. Captured-email aggregation, scorecard-driven segmentation, VSL above the consultation, cross-brand handoff, Netflix-audience capture. Each piece moves the conversion rate on existing traffic. The Year-1 outcome depends on traffic stability, brand cadence, and operator inputs. Results vary by patient interest, procedure mix, and seasonality. The plan is structural infrastructure, not a return guarantee.

No income claims. No procedure-result promises. No "you will book X consultations per month" framing. The infrastructure raises the floor on the existing audience-to-asset gap. The brands themselves carry the result.
