Phase 1+2

Sheila Nazarian | Email Sequences

Five sequences. Scorecard nurture forked by band. Buyer-tier segmented welcome flow per brand. Cross-brand handoff sequence. Netflix-audience capture nurture. Post-consultation routing.

Every sequence stays inside the regulated-medical register. No procedure-efficacy claims, no cosmetic-outcome promises, no pricing on individual procedures, no "you will look X" framing.

Sequence 01 - Scorecard Nurture (forked by band)

Triggered by scorecard submission. Captures email at the top of the scorecard. Forks into one of four bands at the end of the scorecard. Each band gets a different sequence.

Band 1 - Curious-and-researching

Email 1 (Day 0, send 2 min after scorecard submit).

Subject: Your aesthetic-journey readout, Dr. Nazarian's notes

Body: Three-line readout from the scorecard. Links to one Skin Spot starter regimen recommendation and one practice educational article (no procedure-specific content). Footer disclaimer. No CTA other than "Read the regimen brief."

Email 2 (Day 3).

Subject: One question I get a lot at this stage

Body: Educational answer to the most common cold-band question (the form of the question is set on day-one of the install once the question pattern is observed from the early scorecard data). Routes to the Skin Spot home-regimen page. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 7).

Subject: When the home regimen is the next right step

Body: Educational framing on when a home regimen is the next right step before any in-person treatment. Routes to the Skin Spot scorecard surface for product-specific recommendations. Footer disclaimer.

Email 4 (Day 14).

Subject: An invite to come in when you are ready

Body: Soft invitation to book a Spa26 consultation when the home regimen has had time to land. Routes to the Spa26 booking page. Footer disclaimer.

Band 2 - Ready-for-non-surgical

Email 1 (Day 0).

Subject: Your Spa26 fit-readout from Dr. Nazarian

Body: Three-line scorecard readout. Links to the Spa26 booking page. Pre-consultation prep-doc link (what to expect from the consultation, what to bring, what gets covered). Footer disclaimer.

Email 2 (Day 2).

Subject: What a Spa26 consultation actually covers

Body: Educational walkthrough of the consultation process at Spa26 in the regulated-medical register. Routes back to the booking page. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 5).

Subject: The home regimen side of the non-surgical path

Body: Educational framing on how a Skin Spot home regimen supports a non-surgical in-person plan. Routes to the Skin Spot scorecard. Footer disclaimer.

Email 4 (Day 10).

Subject: Holding the booking page open

Body: Soft re-invite to the Spa26 booking page with a clean CTA. Footer disclaimer.

Band 3 - Ready-for-surgical-consultation

Email 1 (Day 0).

Subject: Your consultation-readout from Dr. Nazarian's team

Body: Three-line scorecard readout. Links to the practice consultation form (with the VSL above the form per Piece 02). Pre-consultation prep-doc link. Footer disclaimer.

Email 2 (Day 2).

Subject: What an in-person versus virtual consultation looks like

Body: Educational walkthrough of the difference between in-person and virtual consultations at Nazarian Plastic Surgery. Routes back to the consultation form. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 4).

Subject: Questions that surface during the consultation

Body: Educational framing on what gets covered during the consultation. No procedure-specific content. Routes to the consultation form. Footer disclaimer.

Email 4 (Day 7).

Subject: When the timing is right

Body: Soft re-invite to the consultation page with the VSL above the form. Footer disclaimer.

Band 4 - Returning-patient-maintenance

Email 1 (Day 0).

Subject: Welcome back from the team at Nazarian

Body: Warm-thread re-engagement. Routes to a returning-patient surface that surfaces Spa26 maintenance, Skin Spot home regimen, and practice follow-up consultation as three options. Footer disclaimer.

Email 2 (Day 7).

Subject: One thing the returning-patient calendar tends to miss

Body: Educational framing on the seasonal maintenance cadence and how the three brands work together for returning patients. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 14).

Subject: The Skin Spot regimen check-in

Body: Routes to the Skin Spot scorecard with a returning-patient context pre-loaded. Footer disclaimer.

Subject: Your Spa26 booking is in, here is what is next

Email 2 (Day 2 before appointment).

Subject: The 48-hour prep note

Body: Pre-treatment prep guidance in the regulated-medical register. Routes to the Spa26 site for any additional questions. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 2 after appointment).

Subject: Recovery cadence and the home-care complement

Body: Post-treatment guidance in the regulated-medical register. Routes to the Skin Spot home-regimen page tagged with the post-Spa26 context. Footer disclaimer.

Email 4 (Day 14 after appointment).

Subject: An invite to the Skin Spot home-regimen check

Body: Cross-brand handoff to The Skin Spot. Routes to the Skin Spot scorecard with a "post-Spa26" context pre-loaded. Footer disclaimer.

Email 5 (Day 30 after appointment).

Subject: When the next Spa26 cadence is the right step

Body: Soft re-booking framing in the regulated-medical register. Routes to the Spa26 booking page. Footer disclaimer.

Subject: The monthly regimen-check cadence

Sequence 04 - Cross-Brand Handoff Sequence

Triggered by any high-intent event across the three commercial surfaces (consultation request on the practice site, booking on Spa26, order over a threshold on The Skin Spot). The sequence layers on top of the per-brand welcome flow and routes the captured contact across the cluster.

Email A (sent 14 days after the trigger event, across all three triggers).

Subject: The other two brands in Dr. Nazarian's stack

Body: One-paragraph framing of the active commercial trio (Nazarian Plastic Surgery, Spa26, The Skin Spot) and how they work together. One routed CTA per brand, with the brand the contact already engaged removed. Footer disclaimer.

Email B (sent 30 days after the trigger event).

Subject: The monthly cross-brand cadence

Body: Routes to the monthly cross-brand newsletter signup with the contact pre-tagged by source. Footer disclaimer.

Triggered by scorecard submission on the Netflix-audience capture surface (Piece 05). Source-tag: "netflix."

Email 1 (Day 0).

Subject: From Skin Decision to Dr. Nazarian's full stack

Body: Series-anchored framing in the speaker voice. Three-line scorecard readout. Routes to the right band's brand surface per the scorecard result. Footer disclaimer.

Email 2 (Day 3).

Subject: The three commercial brands behind the series

Body: One-paragraph framing of the active commercial trio. Routed CTAs per brand. Footer disclaimer.

Email 3 (Day 7).

Subject: The home-regimen step the series did not cover

Body: Educational framing on the home-regimen layer. Routes to The Skin Spot scorecard. Footer disclaimer.

Email 4 (Day 14).

Subject: When the in-person Spa26 step is the right step

Body: Soft re-invite to Spa26 with the Netflix-source context pre-loaded. Footer disclaimer.

Email 5 (Day 30).

Subject: The monthly cross-brand newsletter

Body: Re-invite to the monthly cross-brand newsletter signup. Footer disclaimer.

Footer Disclaimer (on every email across every sequence)

Educational content only. Not regulated medical advice. Cosmetic procedures carry inherent risks; outcomes vary by patient and procedure. Consult a board-certified medical professional in your jurisdiction.

What These Sequences Do Not Do

  • Quote any procedure pricing
  • Promise any cosmetic outcome
  • Promise any procedure-efficacy result
  • Claim any superlative ranking
  • Use any banned vocabulary from the Copy Strategy file
  • Use em-dashes anywhere
  • Use minute-quantified CTAs in the cold scorecard nurture (the surface CTA is the only CTA)
  • Use household-tier-name comparisons
  • Reference internal vault filenames or memory entries

Re-Engagement Cadence

Contacts that go cold on any sequence enter a quarterly re-engagement cadence anchored to the monthly cross-brand newsletter. No aggressive "we miss you" framing. Educational content, regulated-medical register, soft re-invite to the scorecard or to the brand surface that fits the journey-stage tag last set.
